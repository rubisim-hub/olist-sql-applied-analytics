# Key Findings

1. **The business generated strong overall sales volume**, with **96,660 total orders** and **15,043,563.24** in gross merchandise value (GMV), indicating meaningful scale for customer, seller, and logistics analysis.

2. **Average order value was relatively stable at 160.58**, while the **maximum single order reached 13,604.08**, suggesting a mostly mid-ticket marketplace with occasional very high-value purchases.

3. **Monthly demand scaled rapidly over time**, growing from **2 orders in 2016-09** to **7,421 orders in 2017-11**, showing strong marketplace expansion before stabilizing at high volume in 2018.

4. **A small number of product categories drove a large share of revenue**:  
   - **health_beauty** led with **1,437,665.78**  
   - **watches_gifts** reached **1,298,292.47**  
   - **bed_bath_table** generated **1,240,386.13**  
   This suggests category concentration around high-demand consumer segments.

5. **Customer repeat behavior was limited**, with only **2,888 repeat customers**, indicating the business likely depended more on new-customer acquisition than on strong repeat purchase frequency.

6. **Order fulfillment performance was strong overall**, with **97.02% of orders delivered**, while canceled (**0.63%**) and unavailable (**0.61%**) orders remained low.

7. **Delivery performance was better than the promised timeline on average**: actual delivery averaged **12.50 days** versus **24.37 estimated days**. However, **8.11% of delivered orders still arrived late**, leaving room for operational improvement.

8. **Delivery quality varied significantly by state**. Late-delivery rates were highest in **AL (23.93%)**, **MA (19.67%)**, and **PI (15.97%)**, while major states performed better, including **SP (5.89%)**, **MG (5.62%)**, and **PR (5.00%)**.

9. **Seller revenue concentration was moderate rather than extreme**: the **top 10 sellers contributed 2,023,089.10 GMV**, equal to **12.88%** of total GMV. This indicates a marketplace that is not overly dependent on a tiny seller group.

10. **Customer experience issues were concentrated in specific categories**. The lowest-rated categories included **office_furniture (3.49)**, **fashion_male_clothing (3.66)**, and **fixed_telephony (3.71)**. Even high-volume categories such as **bed_bath_table** (**9,295 reviewed orders**, **3.90 avg rating**) and **furniture_decor** (**6,375**, **3.92**) showed below-excellent satisfaction, suggesting scale does not automatically translate into better customer experience.

11. **Payment behavior was dominated by credit cards**, which accounted for **78,519 payment rows** and **12,428,884.19** in payment value, far ahead of **boleto (19,784 rows; 2,860,831.27)**. This highlights the importance of card-based checkout optimization and installment-friendly payment flows.

12. **The dataset is suitable for downstream ML use cases**, as the project successfully built an order-level feature layer including purchase month, order GMV, freight, seller count, payment installments, review score, late-delivery flag, and low-review flag.
